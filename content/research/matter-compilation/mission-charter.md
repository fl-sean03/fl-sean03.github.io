## Mission

Close the loop between design and physical reality. Then compress it until they converge.

## End State

A system where you can describe what you want a physical thing to do, and it exists, designed, built, tested, qualified, with the same speed and fluidity we expect from software today.

## The Loop

A compiler isn't a pipeline. It's a loop.

```
    Design ──→ Simulate ──→ Make ──→ Measure
       ↑                                 │
       └──────────── Learn ──────────────┘
```

Today this loop takes 10-20 years for a new material. Thousands of papers, years of process development, more years of qualification. Most discoveries die somewhere in the loop, a material that works in simulation but can't be synthesized, a synthesis that works once but can't be repeated, a product that's never qualified.

We compress the loop to hours. Then we expand what fits inside it, from materials to devices to systems to infrastructure.

Every revolution of the loop:
- Produces a physical result
- Generates knowledge that improves the next revolution
- Gets faster and more autonomous

**Two imperatives:**
1. **Compress the loop**: make each revolution faster, more autonomous, more reliable
2. **Expand the scope**: make the loop work for more materials, more complex structures, larger scales

## The Core Correction

Universal physical production will not arrive as one magical atom printer. It will emerge as a **stack of capabilities across scales**. For some domains, atomically precise control matters. For many others, the winning path is multi-scale control through inverse design, autonomous experimentation, manufacturing intelligence, and automated assembly.

The loop changes character by scale. At small scales it's precise synthesis and patterning. At large scales it's modular orchestration and validated assembly. But it's always the same loop: design, simulate, make, measure, learn.

## The Five Capability Layers

These aren't a sequence. They're the components that make the loop possible at different scales:

| Layer | Domain | Role in the Loop | Horizon |
|-------|--------|-----------------|---------|
| **1. Atomic / Molecular Control** | APM, atomic-scale devices, nanoscale assembly | Finest-grain tools for make + measure | Longest (narrow domains now, general later) |
| **2. Materials Intelligence** | Foundation models, inverse design, autonomous experimentation | Powers the design + simulate steps | Now (this is where the loop first closes autonomously) |
| **3. Manufacturing Knowledge** | Process development, recipes, metrology, failure modes | Connects design to repeatable production, the gap that breaks most loops today | The critical gap, documented by MGI, NIST, 141 ANSI standardization gaps |
| **4. Production Systems** | Digital thread, digital twins, robotics workcells, QC | Where the loop reaches factory scale | Emerging (Genesis Mission, NIST, SMART USA) |
| **5. Infrastructure Assembly** | Modular manufacturing, robotic assembly, digital construction | Where the loop reaches built systems | Far (NASA metamaterial work, modular construction show the flavor) |

## Near-Term Wedge

Build the **first fully autonomous design-make-measure-learn loop for materials**.

Self-driving labs are the prototype. MatterGen + A-Lab + NIST AFL show the pieces exist. We build the complete loop, where AI designs a material, simulation validates it, a lab synthesizes it, instruments characterize it, and the results flow back to improve the next design. All autonomously. All in one system.

Why this wedge:
- Self-driving labs are proving autonomous loops work right now
- Materials science + AI is the core expertise
- The loop produces immediate value (novel materials, discovery services, manufacturing knowledge)
- Each loop revolution generates knowledge that expands the next
- National programs (Genesis Mission, MGI, Manufacturing USA) are actively funding this exact convergence

## Non-Goals

We are not:
- A generic AI lab
- A materials-screening company that just proposes candidates without closing the loop
- A lab-automation toy that just speeds up experiments
- A consultancy
- A point-solution MES vendor

## Decision Filter

Every project gets judged by one question: **Does this compress the loop or expand its scope?**

- Compresses the loop: do it
- Expands what the loop can handle: do it
- Neither: cut it, defer it, or treat it as cash-flow support
