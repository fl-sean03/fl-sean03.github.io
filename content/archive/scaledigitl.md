Every mid-market company is being told they need to "adopt AI." Most adoption projects are scoped as multi-quarter transformations led by consultancies that bill by the hour and deliver slides. The actual AI-enabled workflow is two engineers and a weekend of prompt engineering.

**The thesis:** Productize the prompt-engineering weekend. Fixed-scope engagements: pick a workflow, wire it up, ship it in two weeks. No slides, no steering committees, no discovery phase. Price it like a SaaS onboarding fee.

**What was built:** Agency landing page at [scaledigitl.com](https://www.scaledigitl.com) with hero, positioning, and CTA.

**Why it's parked:** Consulting is a sales-heavy business and I hate sales. The thesis is right: there's money in the gap between "need AI" and "hire Accenture." Someone with a network and a patience for enterprise procurement should run it.
