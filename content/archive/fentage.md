Most apparel brands live or die on volume. The ones that don't — Loro Piana, Brunello Cucinelli — win by making the product itself feel like an institution. Narrow lane, high craft, memberships and ateliers instead of drops and discounts.

**The thesis:** There's a gap between streetwear (loud, cheap, seasonal) and heritage luxury (expensive, stuffy, old). A brand with structured silhouettes, atelier-style customization, and a membership layer could sit in that gap for buyers who want quiet craft without the heritage price tag.

**What was built:** Full brand site with pillars, collections, atelier services, and membership copy at [fentage.com](https://fentage.com).

**Why it's parked:** I don't know how to run an apparel company and I don't want to learn. The positioning is the interesting part; the execution is someone else's job.
