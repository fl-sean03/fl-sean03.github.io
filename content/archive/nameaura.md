Naming things is one of the two hard problems in computer science and also in startups. Every founder spends a weekend with a thesaurus and a domain lookup tool, comes up with something like "Zendrix" or "Flowlet," registers it, and moves on. The process is slow and the output is usually worse than asking a language model.

**The thesis:** A good naming tool isn't a thesaurus. It's a language model that understands positioning, checks domain availability in real time, and generates names that sound like real companies instead of Scrabble racks.

**What was built:** Working app at nameaura.co. Enter positioning, get candidate names with live domain availability. Screenshot below.

**Why it's parked:** The category is crowded (Namelix, Shopify name generator, Namecheap beast mode) and the product is a feature, not a company. Fine utility, small moat. The site has since been taken down; the idea is the only thing left.
