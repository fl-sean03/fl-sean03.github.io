The frame most people use for AI agents — "a tool you delegate tasks to" — is too small. An agent with its own wallet, its own goals, and continuous existence is closer to an economic actor than a tool. What happens when you stop telling it what to do and let it operate?

**The thesis:** An autonomous entity with revenue, expenses, and self-directed priorities is a new kind of thing. Not a company, not an employee, not a chatbot. It negotiates, saves, invests, pursues goals over months. The interesting question is what it would choose to do.

**What was built:** Brand site at [opspawn.com](https://opspawn.com) with the "autonomous AI entity" positioning, seed infrastructure (hibernated snapshot on Hetzner), early experiments with self-directed workflows.

**Why it's parked:** Subsumed into the broader autonomous-agents fleet work. OpSpawn as a standalone identity didn't need to exist — the interesting experiments happen across the whole fleet, not inside one branded container. The seed is hibernated, recoverable, and may come back as a sub-persona rather than a standalone.
