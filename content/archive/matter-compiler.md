---
title: "Matter Compiler"
description: "Compilation pipeline from materials specification to manufacturing instructions"
date: 2026-01-15
---

Software has compilers. Hardware has EDA. Materials has spreadsheets and tribal knowledge. A materials spec — "this alloy, this microstructure, these tolerances" — gets translated to a process plan by humans, every time, from scratch.

**The thesis:** If the target microstructure is computable from the spec, and the process-structure relationship is learnable, then the translation from "what I want" to "how to make it" can be compiled the same way source code compiles to machine instructions. The output isn't g-code — it's a process plan with predicted yield and tolerance envelopes.

**Why it's parked:** Subsumed by the broader Groundstate thesis. Matter Compiler was one layer of the stack; the vertically integrated company is the better frame. The compiler idea will resurface as an internal tool, not a standalone product.
